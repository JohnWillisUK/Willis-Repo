Me and my running journey

Run director, endurance runner, slow runner and cake aficionado – just a few of the things I like to think I am. I’m John Willis, a runner from Selby and across this site I wish to portray to you my love of running and the awesome things that come with it.

I found a love of running at school running cross country in a club known as the ‘160 club’. The purpose of the club was to challenge yourself to run 160km (100 miles) within 5 years.

After a break from running that lasted 16 years, I have regained that love and in 2021 I plan to run a minimum 160km in a 24 hours event: Endure 24. You can find me in all the coolest running spots including: Strava, Run Britain rankings as well as my popular Instagram page - @Runandeatcake.

My running world

As a non-runner you could easily look at runners and think that all there is to running is putting one foot in front of the other. While that could be true - in my experience it couldn’t be further from the truth. Running as a hobby for me includes volunteering, eating cake, friends, helping my community and occasionally putting one foot in front of the other.

  • parkrun

    My first parkrun was on 5th June 2010, I then went seven years before the bug bit me. I have now ran 55 times and volunteered on 49 occasions. I’ve been a run director at Selby parkrun for 3 years and an event director at Brayton Academy junior parkrun for 6 months.

  • Selby Striders

    Selby Striders is my local run club who I have ran with for around 3 years. When I first joined, I was new to the area and knew no one. I now am part of a massive community of runners and this is massively thanks to the Striders.

  • Vale of York Athletics Club (VOYAC)

    VOYAC is a new club I’m setting up. At VOYAC we offer structured training based around the 80/20 training method and I’ll be training here with one eye on my aspiration of 160 km in 24 hours next summer.

  • Ultra-Marathons

    My favourite runs are ultras-marathons. This means any run further than 26.2 miles - the distance of a marathon. But more on my endurance runs later.
